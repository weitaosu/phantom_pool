## About the Sponsor: TRON

Tron is an open-source, decentralised, Layer 1 blockchain offering a fast, developer-friendly way for anyone, anywhere, to build and transact onchain.

## **Brief Description of Bounty**

Build a comprehensive, real-world payment or DeFi product demo on TRON that goes beyond simple token transfers and shows a complete user journey, strong UX, and a practical business or consumer use case.

## Prize Information

$1,000

## Objective

Create an open-ended payments or DeFi  project on TRON that demonstrates a clear “product”, not just contracts. Your demo must show an end-to-end flow that a real user or business would understand and want to use.

Your project can be in any of the following directions (choose one primary direction and execute it deeply):

- Merchant acquiring and checkout
- Affordable consumer payments (P2P) solutions
- B2B payments and invoicing
- Payroll and human capital payments
- Payments service provider tooling (PSP)
- On/Off-ramp experience (demo/simulation acceptable)
- RWA products and solutions with a payments angle (retail-oriented)
- Credit solutions that go beyond simple lending/borrowing
- Liquidity aggregation solutions
- Yield farming / vaults

## Background Information

Payments adoption depends on more than sending tokens. Real payment products need strong UX, clear settlement logic, good failure handling, receipts/invoices, and business-friendly features like reporting, reconciliation, permissions, and customer support flows. This bounty exists to push teams toward complete, retail-oriented or business-oriented payment experiences on TRON.

Your submission must include a working demo that covers these baseline expectations:

**A) Required baseline features (all submissions)**

- A clear user journey with at least two roles (i.e.: payer + merchant, employer + employee, business + vendor, PSP + client).
- A working end-to-end flow that includes:
    1. setup / onboarding step,
    2. the payment action, and
    3. a verifiable outcome.
- **On-chain verifiability**: include transaction hashes and show how they map to UI state (paid/unpaid, settled, claimed, etc.).
- **Clear UX for fees and failure cases**: the app must explain what happens if a transaction fails and what the user should do next.

**B) What will be discarded**

- Simple TRC-20 send/receive demo only (basic wallet transfer UI)
- Basic lending/borrowing dApp with no novel credit/payment concept
- Just a smart contract with no usable demo UX

**C) Innovation expectations for specific directions**

- Credit solutions: Must be meaningfully innovative. Plain deposit/borrow/repay will be rejected.
- RWA with payments angle: Must focus on retail adoption and a full experience. Simple tokenization alone will be rejected.

**D) Network + code**

- Must run on TRON testnet (Nile preferred).
- Must be open source with a clear README and reproducible steps.

## Jugement Criteria

- Product completeness: feels like a real payments product, clear roles and flow, usable UI.
- Real-world usefulness: solves a real merchant/consumer/business payments problem.
- Innovation: novel idea or meaningful improvement beyond standard demos.
- Technical correctness: transactions match app logic, verifiable on-chain, solid architecture.
- UX clarity + edge cases: good fee/failure messaging, handles common pitfalls well.
- Polish: clean README, easy setup, good demo quality.
